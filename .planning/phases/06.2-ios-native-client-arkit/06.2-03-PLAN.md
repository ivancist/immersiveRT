<objective>
Build the two device-tier pieces: the CoreMotion orientation source (real OS-fused attitude quaternion per D-09) and the 5-second heartbeat timer (PHONE-06).

Per D-09, CoreMotion supplies REAL orientation data (`CMDeviceMotion.attitude.quaternion`) — this is small, self-contained, and not wasted when 06.3 later swaps in ARKit. Position, gesture displacement, and driftConfidence are NOT computed here; they remain zero-stubbed in the encoder (Plan 02) per D-01/D-09 — no Madgwick/ZUPT/Kalman port (SENS-01..05 out of scope). No ARKit (D-01).

The direct `CMQuaternion(x,y,z,w) → (qw,qx,qy,qz)` mapping using `.xArbitraryZVertical` is RESEARCH's reasoned starting point, NOT a verified answer — its on-device correctness is gated by the axis-verification checkpoint in Plan 09 (Pitfall 1). This plan wires the mapping and flags it clearly in code.

Purpose: Feed real orientation into the byte-identical encoder; keep the slot alive with heartbeats.
Output: `CoreMotionSource.swift`, `HeartbeatTimer.swift`, and heartbeat tests.
</objective>
