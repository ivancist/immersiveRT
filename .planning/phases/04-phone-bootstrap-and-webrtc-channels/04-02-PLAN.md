<objective>
Deliver the core connection slice: after pairing, the phone fans out one unreliable WebRTC data
channel to every desktop in the room, both endpoints confirm each channel to the server, and the
server fires the reliable `player-ready` game-start gate once all channels are established. The
phone's connecting view counts up live and flips to the active/ready view.

Purpose: Satisfy PHONE-03 end-to-end — real RTCPeerConnections with the locked
`{ordered:false, maxRetransmits:0}` semantics, two-sided channel-readiness tracking on the server,
and the `player-ready` broadcast (D-08/D-09). Includes the minimal desktop answerer in room.js so
the two-sided confirmation can actually complete and be observed (RESEARCH A4; full desktop receive
/ target-state store is Phase 6 DESK-02).
Output: `channel_ready` tracking + `handle_rtc_channel_ready` + `route_to_phone` on the server, the
WebRTC offerer + player-ready handling in phone.js, and the WebRTC answerer in room.js.
</objective>

## Phase Goal (user story)

**As a** phone player joining a room via a QR scan, **I want to** grant motion access and connect
to every desktop in the room over unreliable WebRTC data channels while keeping my session alive,
**so that** my phone becomes a live low-latency motion controller the moment the game signals
`player-ready`.

> This plan delivers the connection slice: paired → all channels open → `player-ready` → active view.

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-04-03 | Tampering | ICE candidate / offer injection in signaling | high | mitigate | Server attestation via routing envelope (D-03) plus the existing `envelope.from` != connection-id drop guard in wt_server/ws_server prevents source spoofing; desktop trusts the server's routing, not the offer contents |
