## Phase Goal

**As a** third-party game developer, **I want to** call `connect()` and `joinRoom()` against the SDK, **so that** I can establish the platform transport and join a room without reimplementing WebTransport/WebSocket signaling.

> Slice: the SDK owns the signaling pipe. After this plan a consumer can connect (WT-first/WS-fallback) and join a room programmatically, receiving the join-ack data to render its own lobby/QR (D-01). WebRTC peer fan-out + the decode pipeline land in 07-05; here the signaling backbone and its request/response shapes are extracted verbatim and wired to Platform.

<objective>
Extract room.ts's transport primitives — WebTransport connect/register, the `sendWtRequest`/`sendWtMessage` bidi-stream helpers, WS connect with the pending-message queue, the server-message dispatcher, `signalSend`, and `leaveRoom`'s transport teardown — into `packages/immersive-rt/src/transport/connection.ts`, preserving the WT-first/WS-fallback ordering (D-06). Wire `Platform.connect()`/`joinRoom()`/`reconnect()`/`leaveRoom()` on top, starting the tick from `connect()` (D-08). Forward session messages to an `onSignal` consumer callback so the client keeps its lobby/session UI (ASVS V2/V3 stays consumer-side).

Purpose: The signaling connection is the shared pipe carrying both session messages (for the game's UI) and RTC signaling (consumed internally in 07-05). This plan lands the pipe and its public connect/join surface.
Output: connection.ts (signaling only), Platform connect/join/reconnect/leave, and a test of the send/dispatch/onSignal seam.
</objective>
